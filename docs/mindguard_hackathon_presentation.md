# MindGuard Hackathon Deck (10 Slides)

Use this as your exact slide script. Keep each slide visual-first with minimal text.

## Slide 1 - Title
**MindGuard**  
Detect stress before it breaks you

- Team name and members (optional)
- Hero visual: person at desk + subtle stress waveform overlay
- Footer: Hackathon name and date

## Slide 2 - Problem Statement
- Mental stress and burnout often go unnoticed
- People hesitate to seek help
- Early warning signs are hidden in behavior

Key line (centered):
**"Mental breakdown does not start when you feel it. It starts when your behavior changes."**

Visual idea:
- Timeline graphic: Hidden stress buildup -> Visible breakdown

## Slide 3 - Limits of Existing Solutions
- Chatbots are reactive
- Mood trackers need manual check-ins
- No early detection of hidden stress
- Limited personalization

Visual idea:
- 2-column comparison cards:
  - Existing tools: reactive, manual, delayed
  - Need: passive, proactive, personalized

## Slide 4 - Our Solution
**Passive AI-driven mental well-being monitoring**

- Detects hidden behavioral and emotional signals
- Generates early warnings
- Gives timely interventions

Visual idea:
- Single sentence in bold + 3 icon cards (detect, score, intervene)

## Slide 5 - System Overview (Multi-Modal Intelligence)
Group the system into three blocks:

1. Behavioral Signals
- Typing pattern analysis
- Screen time tracking

2. Emotional Signals (Simulated)
- Voice stress signal
- Facial emotion signal

3. Contextual Signals
- Age, role, profession
- Activity/chat history patterns

Visual idea:
- 3-column block diagram with colored headers and icons

## Slide 6 - How It Works (Architecture)
Flow:
**Input -> Processing -> Output**

Input:
- Behavioral + emotional + contextual signals

Processing:
- Lightweight AI + rule-based weighted scoring
- Pattern detection and risk analysis

Output:
- Stress score (0-100)
- Risk level (Low / Medium / High)
- Smart intervention suggestion

Visual idea:
- Horizontal pipeline with arrows and color badges

## Slide 7 - Demo / UI Screens
Show 4 screenshots/placeholders:
- Input dashboard
- Stress analysis result
- Color-coded risk indicator
- Stress trend graph over time

Demo talk track (one line):
**"From raw signals to actionable intervention in one click."**

## Slide 8 - Novelty / Innovation
- Multi-modal passive detection system
- No dependency on self-reporting
- Behavioral + emotional + contextual fusion
- Key innovation: intervention timing

Key line:
**"We do not wait for users to say they are stressed. We detect it before they realize it."**

Visual idea:
- Innovation badge + timing dial graphic

## Slide 9 - Impact
- Helps students avoid burnout
- Supports professionals in managing stress
- Enables early intervention
- Improves productivity and well-being

Visual idea:
- Audience icons (student, employee, institution) with outcomes

## Slide 10 - Feasibility & Future Scope
Feasibility:
- Uses lightweight, non-sensitive data
- Easy to implement and scale
- Works with simulated or real signals

Future scope:
- Wearable integrations
- Real-time continuous monitoring
- College/company deployment
- Advanced predictive analytics

Closing line:
**"MindGuard makes preventive mental wellness practical, scalable, and human-centered."**

## Visual Style Guide (for PPT/Figma/Canva)
- Theme: clean dark navy or clean light gray (pick one and stay consistent)
- Accent colors:
  - High risk: `#E74C3C`
  - Medium risk: `#F1C40F`
  - Low risk: `#27AE60`
  - Primary accent: `#1F6FEB`
- Fonts: Poppins / Manrope / Sora (one heading + one body font)
- Rule: max 4 bullets per slide, max 8 words per bullet when possible
- Use icons on every content slide, no dense paragraphs
- Keep generous spacing and large visual hierarchy
