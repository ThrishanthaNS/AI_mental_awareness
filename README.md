# AI_mental_awareness

Problem Statement + Solution :
In today’s digital and academic environments, early signs of mental stress, burnout, and emotional decline among students and young professionals often go unnoticed because individuals either fail to recognize these patterns themselves or hesitate to actively seek help, leading to worsening mental health outcomes; existing solutions rely heavily on self-reporting or generic chat-based interactions, which lack timely detection and personalization, so we propose to build a passive, AI-driven mental well-being monitoring system that analyzes behavioral signals such as typing patterns, screen time, academic workload, and simulated activity trends to detect hidden stress indicators without requiring explicit user input, generates a dynamic mental risk score, identifies patterns of burnout or emotional decline over time, and provides context-aware, timely interventions such as “sell now vs wait”-style decisions for mental energy (e.g., suggesting breaks, task rescheduling, or coping strategies at peak stress moments), all through a clean dashboard that visualizes trends, risk levels, and actionable insights while ensuring privacy by processing minimal and non-sensitive data, thereby transforming mental health support from reactive conversation-based systems into proactive, behavior-driven decision support tools that are scalable, non-intrusive, and impactful for real-world use
